# Critique: Remaining Gaps in the Architecture and Specification

*Non-normative. A gap catalogue for the current state of [verification-maximal-os.md](verification-maximal-os.md) and its companions, written after the previous critique's numbered contradictions and rearchitecture candidates (A–E) were all addressed and retired.*

The earlier critique found internal contradictions: places where the cross-reference machinery had papered over a real inconsistency. Those are gone: the admission-test discipline is now applied uniformly, the delete-rather-than-defend rule is consistent, and every mechanism-level tension it raised has been booked, resolved, or honestly declined. What remains is not contradiction but a different set of gaps: where the spec is **silent**, where a residual is **acknowledged but not closed**, where the limit is **irreducible**, and where the **artifact itself** has outgrown the review gate that is supposed to guard it. They are numbered continuously for reference and sorted by kind, not severity. The single most important gap (13) is that essentially none of it is built.

## Unbooked: the spec is silent or self-contradictory here

**1. Emergency calling contradicts the verified radio posture.** §12 makes *no downgrade, no null cipher, mutual authentication (5G-AKA)* a **verified property** of the L2/L3 servers, and §15 removes 2G/3G/4G from the silicon so downgrade is "unexpressible." But emergency calling (E911/112) is legally mandated to connect a device with **no SIM, no valid credential**, and, across deployed networks, frequently only over a legacy generation or an unauthenticated *limited-service / emergency-attach* path that by construction has no mutual authentication and may use a null or minimal cipher. A stack that *cannot express* an unauthenticated attach cannot place an emergency call where only such attach exists, and a radio with no 2G/3G/4G cannot fall back where that is the only coverage. This is the deep-sleep-versus-cellular contradiction one layer over: either specify an attested, capability-narrowed **emergency-attach exception** to the verified no-downgrade property (and book the surface it reopens on the most-attacked stack), or state explicitly that emergency calling over legacy or unauthenticated networks is **out of scope**. That is a serious stance for a phone-shaped device to take by silence rather than by decision.

**2. Availability is never treated as a security property, yet the design fails closed everywhere.** Thermal trip is fail-stop (§15), watchdog bite is reset (§16), an admission failure refuses code (§13), a radio crash costs connectivity until restart (§16), duress crypto-erases (§9). Each is correct alone; together they make **induced-fault denial-of-service cheap**: an adversary who can provoke faults, thermal events, or crash-restart loops degrades or drops service at will. The spec's answer, "bounded downtime, not permanent DoS" (§16), is an *integrity* guarantee, not an availability one. Against the hostile radio infrastructure the threat model already claims to defend (§3), infrastructure whose goal is often precisely to keep the device off the network or off an emergency call, availability-under-attack is first-class, and it is budgeted nowhere (detection and containment latency are; availability is not).

**3. Consent comprehension is outside every theorem, and it is the dominant real attack.** §8/§12 make the powerbox grant *unspoofable* (robust delimited declassification, an RoT secure-attention indicator, an input capability routed to a small verified agent), closing the **mechanism** side completely. But the residual §17 files under "the user consented to the wrong thing" is not a footnote for this device: duress credentials and biometric vaults imply a high-risk user, and social-engineering a correctly-rendered, genuinely-attested prompt is exactly how such users fall. There is no mechanism *below* the prompt: no grant rate-limiting, no anomaly detection on grant patterns, no comprehension scaffolding, no notion that a flurry of individually-valid consents is itself suspicious. *Unspoofable* the spec nails; *comprehensible and abuse-resistant* it does not address.

**4. Duress is a countermeasure with unmodeled human factors.** The duress credential crypto-erases rather than unlocks (§9/§12), defending future recoverability, but it is **irreversibly destructive on accidental entry** (booked), does nothing once keys are resident in an active session (the lock-state seam, §17), and, most importantly, its *existence as a known feature* invites the coercion it defends: an adversary aware the platform offers duress compels *both* credentials or watches for the erase. The spec treats duress as a clean mechanism; its actual protection is a human-protocol property it does not model, and overclaiming it endangers the very user who relies on it.

**5. Anti-rollback freshness rests on a hand-drawn criticality line.** §17 surrenders freshness for the mutable user-data volume "by design," carrying the *security-relevant* low-rate state (version floor, key revocation, credential generation, lockout counter) on the RoT monotonic counter instead. The split is right, but *which* state is security-critical is a manual classification, and a misclassification silently loses freshness on something that mattered, with no proof to catch it (a §17 spec-gap of exactly the crown-jewel kind). The RoT-fresh set should be **enumerated normatively and argued closed**, not left to per-feature judgment.

## Booked but open: acknowledged residuals the design has not closed

**6. The single-mechanism concentration is elegant and undiversified.** Deleting the MMU, PMP, the S/U rings, and now the IOMMU (§7/§15) leaves **CHERI as the sole in-band mechanism** for spatial isolation, privilege, *and* DMA, with no disjoint backstop anywhere. §17 books this as *verify rather than hedge*, and the argument is coherent, but a single CHERI **logic** error (in monotonicity, revocation, or the tag plane), as distinct from a fab defect, is a **total and simultaneous** failure of every isolation boundary on the machine, and G2 ("compromising any component yields only its authority") does not survive it. The hedge is CHERI's own formal verification, which does not yet exist at this scale (13). Minimal-mechanism elegance has been chosen over defense-in-depth at the one layer where defense-in-depth is classically most prized; a defensible bet, but a bet, and it belongs in the spec as a first-order architectural stance, not only in the residual list.

**7. Full remediation stays proof-gated on the most-attacked surface.** §16 now honestly splits **containment** (fast, monotone authority-narrowing, no proof) from **remediation** (proof-gated) and budgets the degraded window between them. The honesty is welcome; the gap is real: a live zero-day in the RRC/NAS parser is answered by dropping the radio to a safe subset or fail-stop, then waiting machine-hours-to-days for a re-proved parser to clear generation admission (§11). Mean-time-to-*restored-function* is still proof latency. This is the accepted price of refusing unproven hot-patches (not a flaw), but it is not closed, and it **compounds gap 2**: an attacker who can repeatedly force the degraded subset achieves a durable partial DoS under the cover of "containment worked."

**8. Protocol-level security sits above the proof frontier.** The crypto reductions reach *scheme* level (IND-CCA/EUF-CMA); §17 states plainly that TLS 1.3, WireGuard, and the cellular AKA composition is "a further layer this guarantee does not reach." §12's Bertie/hax and miTLS give binary-level memory safety and *bonus* protocol proofs, but the **composed session-security theorem**, the property a user actually leans on when the design says *hyper-secure*, is not among the crown jewels. This is the largest scope boundary that marketing word crosses without a proof behind it, and it should be named in §3's Defended/Residual split, not only deep in §17.

**9. Physical and sub-partition side channels grow more load-bearing as the device model gets more adversarial.** Probe-level power/EM analysis, TEMPEST emission coupling, RF fingerprinting for link-layer unlinkability, and the LPDDR6 sub-channel die-internal coupling are all booked (§17) and none is closed. The *seized-and-once-unlocked* and *duress* cases (§3) put a physical adversary in scope by design, which raises the weight of precisely these residuals; the new event-driven sensor readout (§12) adds a data-dependent-timing surface the island partition is *argued* to contain but that widens what an on-die or physical observer can probe. These are honest ceilings, but load-bearing ones for the physical-adversary cases the threat model now claims.

## Irreducible: real, and no design closes them

**10. Specification error is still first-ranked, and the crown-jewel set is compounding.** Every hardening added a spec whose wrongness no proof catches: the delimited-release bound and robust-declassification statement (§8), the ASN.1/UPER codec (§12), the dedup keyed-digest interface (§10), the initialization-tag invariant (§15), the WCET latency magnitudes, the `Zkt`/`Zvkt` leakage model, the Ztso and static-prediction fetch models, every IDL wire-format mapping, and each Lustre control program. The residual is unchanged in *kind* but larger in *extent* than at the previous critique: the design became safer by adding correct-by-construction machinery, and each piece is a new small oracle that must be **stated** correctly. Not a flaw to fix, but a cost to track, and it is growing.

**11. Fabrication trust is now evidence-based, not proof-based.** IRIS backside optical verification (§17) genuinely turns fab from an act of faith into obtainable evidence, and single-die integration helps, but it resolves coarser structure than the smallest features, it is *evidence, not proof*, and a fab adversary below its resolution stays in scope. The largest post-software residual is narrowed, not removed.

**12. The bedrock assumptions, the human, and the eUICC stay outside the trusted computer.** MLWE/MSIS and ECDLP/CDH are irreducible axioms (§17); the user is outside every consent theorem (gap 3); and the eUICC remains "the one tolerated foreign computer" (§4/§12), contained to zero platform authority but an admitted exception to the design's defining claim. None is a defect; all three are permanent boundaries the *no foreign computers / everything verified* framing should keep stating out loud rather than letting the reader forget.

## Meta: the specification as an artifact

**13. Nothing load-bearing is built, and that is the real security level today.** RTL ⊑ Sail at application-class scale, the certifying Rust→CHERI compiler, the Coq re-proofs of seL4 / GoJournal / VeriBetrFS / RefFS / SFSCQ, the CHERI-TAL soundness metatheorem, the WCET estimator, the relational-Sail constant-time logic: every one is named in-scope and **none exists**. The spec is candid that RTL ⊑ Sail is "the least-built layer of the stack," but the point generalizes: the design's *as-specified* assurance is very high and its *as-existing* assurance is that of an unimplemented research program. The distance between the two is the dominant fact about the system's security and is given no section of its own.

**14. The fresh-proof mass is unprecedented in one prover, and its composition is unproven.** The single-prover discipline (§5) is right for the *checker*, but it concentrates an enormous fresh effort (a verified kernel, filesystem stack, compiler back-end, ISA refinement, crypto-reduction suite, and type-system metatheory, each individually a multi-year artifact) into one Coq development with no cross-community parallelism and no published labor estimate. Worse, the system's security is a **conjunction** of a dozen crown-jewel theorems joined at named seams (NI ⋈ timing, WCET ⋈ isolation, AE ⋈ noninterference, liveness ⋈ schedulability, secure-compilation ⋈ robust-safety, CHERI-TAL ⋈ Sail, CT ⋈ RTL ⊑ Sail). Each seam is booked; there is **no top-level theorem that the composition holds**, and a chain of individually-sound proofs can still fail to compose. The absence of a stated end-to-end composition obligation is the deepest structural gap in the verification argument.

**15. The document has outgrown its own review gate.** §5 makes independent specification review a **release gate** (the primary defense against gap 10, the first-ranked risk), yet the spec is now ~270 KB of dense, deeply cross-referenced prose, roughly double its size at the previous critique, and the old complaint that it is "engineered to defeat review" is more true, not less. The review process meant to catch spec error is itself unspecified: no decomposition into numbered atomic requirements, no reviewer-coverage argument, no separation of the normative kernel from the (excellent, but voluminous) rationale. A spec whose top risk is its own correctness needs a **review methodology as first-class as its proof methodology**, and has none.

---

*A note on what is not here: the mechanism-level architecture is largely settled and internally consistent, and the admission-test framework does the work it claims. Almost every remaining gap is one of **execution, specification, scope-honesty, or human factors**, not of mechanism design. That is the expected shape for a design at this maturity: the hard part left is no longer deciding what to build, but building it, stating it correctly, and being honest about the edges.*
